Computer Science Project 2025

Name of Project

Exam Number:258530

2 Investigation (Approximately 400 words)

1.
 Upon reading the brief, I was relieved that the dataset could be "relevant to your interests," allowing me to work on something I genuinely enjoy. Since music is my passion, I initially explored using publicly available datasets to find meaningful patterns. My first idea was to investigate whether a person’s country of origin correlated with their chances of becoming a famous musician. I envisioned a form where users could input their location and musician status, then compare the number of musicians in their area with those who had made it onto the Billboard Top 100 over the past 50 years.
2.
However, I quickly realized music was not a viable topic due to its lack of clear patterns. Taste is subjective, influenced by countless factors beyond geography. Additionally, relevant data was scarce—most datasets focused on artist statistics rather than broader trends. This led me back to brainstorming, and I turned to Kaggle for inspiration.
3.
While searching, I found a dataset from a U.S. government body detailing bird strikes on aircraft across North America. The sheer volume and depth of this data intrigued me, and it seemed far more suitable for my project. Firstly, graphing the data would be straightforward—either a bird hit a plane, or it didn’t. Secondly, the dataset’s level of detail provided extensive opportunities for analysis. Lastly, this topic resonated with me as both aviation and engineering fascinate me. More importantly, analyzing this data could have real-world benefits, improving air travel safety and wildlife conservation.
4.
One of my key research angles is examining how bird size and breed impact strike likelihood. Could avoiding airport construction in areas with high-risk bird populations reduce collisions? This approach integrates an interactive element I had planned from the beginning—the "bird maker"—which allows users to design a bird and see its probability of being involved in a strike.
5.
As of writing this (6/12/24), I feel excited and motivated by this project. The dataset is of high quality, and programming a tool to predict bird strike probability sounds both fun and meaningful. Beyond entertainment, my research could enhance aviation safety, improve aircraft maintenance, and aid wildlife conservation efforts. With 343 bird species recorded in strikes, the potential impact of this project is significant.
